Braille Botanical Guidebook

Our comprehensive braille guidebook offers an innovative solution to making botanical education accessible for visually impaired visitors. Drawing inspiration from the tactile guidebook at Singapore's Gardens by the Bay, this resource integrates traditional braille with thoughtfully crafted layouts to support an engaging and inclusive learning experience.

Key Features:

  • Tactile Plant Descriptions: Detailed braille text describing plant characteristics, origins, and interesting facts
  • Structured Layout: Organized format that makes navigation easy through touch
  • Educational Content: Covers diverse plant species throughout the Botanic Gardens
  • Complementary Resource: Works alongside the enhanced signage and existing website (for already existing plants)
  • Tactile QR Code Boundary: A raised tactile border surrounds each QR code for easy fingertip detection.

The guidebook serves as both an educational tool and a bridge to independence, allowing visually impaired visitors to explore the gardens at their own pace while gaining deep knowledge about the botanical collections. The QR code leads to the Friends of the Botanics' website for further information on that specific plant.

Enhanced Signage System

Our enhanced signage system transforms traditional plant identification signs into comprehensive accessibility tools. By integrating braille text with existing visual information, we create an inclusive experience that serves all visitors while maintaining the aesthetic integrity of the garden environment.

Key Features:

  • Dual-Format Information: Combines visual text with tactile braille for universal accessibility
  • QR Code Integration: Links to the relevant plant/tree in the Friends Of The Gardens Website
  • Consistent Design Language: Maintains visual harmony with existing garden aesthetics

The enhanced signage serves as a means for the visually impaired to better navigate and understand the garden.

Digital Platform Enhancement

Our digital platform enhancement focuses on making the Friends of Glasgow Botanic Gardens website more accessible through the integration of British Sign Language (BSL) videos. This solution is a complementary resource to our Enhanced Signage solution.

Key Features:

  • BSL Video Integration: Embedded British Sign Language videos providing signed descriptions of plant information
  • QR Code Connectivity: Seamless integration with physical signage through QR codes
  • Multi-Modal Access: Supports various accessibility needs including visual, auditory, and motor impairments

The digital platform serves as a comprehensive resource that complements our physical accessibility solutions, providing visitors with multiple ways to access botanical information.
