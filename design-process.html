Design Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Cost Constraints

Problem: All improvements had to remain low-cost due to reliance on public grants (e.g., National Lottery Heritage Fund).

Solution: Costs analysis on the various solutions suggested was carried out, designs that had a lower projected costs were prioritised. Several ideas with expensive installation or operation costs such as the Braille pathway were dropped for this reason.

Challenge: Manpower Constraints

Problem: The garden operates with only ~20 volunteer guides. Proposed solutions had to either function with minimal staff support or be usable by visitors and/or personal caretakers with no specialist knowledge.

Solution: The Braille guidebook and QR-linked webpage solutions were selected because they empower independent access without requiring additional volunteer staff assistance.

Challenge: Effect on Plants and Trees within the Gardens

Problem: Solutions must not cause damage to the plants, trees, or root systems within the gardens. Solutions that required the extensive transplantation of flora were discouraged.

Solution: The Braille Pathway concept, which required embedding tactile markers into walkways near plant beds, was rejected. It was replaced with a Braille guidebook, which preserved tactile access without invasive ground work.
