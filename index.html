Transforming Accessibility Through Design

Our comprehensive accessibility enhancement project addresses the critical need for inclusive experiences at Glasgow Botanic Gardens, ensuring that visually impaired visitors and those with hearing impairments are not shortchanged in their ability to engage with botanical education and exploration.

Key Project Features

Dedicated Braille Guidebook

Grade 2 braille descriptions with tactile graphics, designed for outdoor durability and professional accessibility standards.

Enhanced Signage System

Enhanced existing signages with braille text translation and QR codes, that links to the existing website with additional information on the plant.

Additional British Sign Language (BSL) support for the website

Embedding BSL videos in the Friends of the Gardens Tree Trail website to accommodate those who prefer BSL descriptions, with future plans to expand this to other trails.
