Additional Interviews

Interview with Elspeth

In our brief discussion with Elspeth, a volunteer who is partially blind herself, we gained insight into how the tour is adapted for visitors with visual impairments. Volunteers are trained to guide blind participants using techniques such as offering their elbow for support and encouraging tactile exploration. While the tours have not yet catered specifically to deaf participants, a typical group for those with impairments would maintain a 1:5 volunteer-to-participant ratio, with a preference for English-speaking attendees. During the tours, participants are encouraged to touch certain plants and even statues to enhance sensory engagement, though precautions are taken with plants that may be harmful, such as cacti or the Venus flytrap. Elspeth also mentioned that there are around 20 volunteers involved in facilitating these tours.


Q&A with Chrissy

During a Question and Answer session with Chrissy, a volunteer at the Botanic Gardens, we explored the current accessibility features and challenges faced by visitors with visual and hearing impairments. Chrissy highlighted that there were no support for the visually and audibly impaired at the gardens. Chrissy also expressed that the Friends of the Botanics are willing to explore potential solutions to enhance accessibility but the cost should be kept to a minimum.
