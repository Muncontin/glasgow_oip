Prototype Evolution

Our prototype evolved through iterative feedback and contextual testing. Initial concepts were reworked into more practical and inclusive solutions, tailored to real-world use in the Glasgow Botanic Gardens.

Initial Concept

Started with a braille map and standalone QR code system linking to a newly developed accessibility website.

Early Iteration

Explored a braille pathway system inspired by tactile ground surface indicators (e.g., traffic stop strips) to aid navigation without visual cues.

Final Implementation

Converged on durable braille signages and a tactile guidebook for wayfinding and plant information. The QR solution was adapted to supplement the existing garden website with BSL video content instead of creating a new platform.
