External Surveys & Public Engagement

Public Accessibility Survey

Participants: 247 respondents | Duration: 4 weeks | Method: Online and in-person

Comprehensive survey assessing current accessibility experiences at public gardens and botanical institutions across Scotland.

Key Findings:

  • 78% of visually impaired respondents avoid botanical gardens due to accessibility barriers
  • 92% expressed interest in tactile information systems
  • 85% prefer self-guided over guided tour experiences
  • 67% use smartphones for accessibility apps regularly

Garden Staff Feedback Survey

Participants: 23 staff members | Duration: 2 weeks | Method: Anonymous online survey

Internal survey gathering insights from garden staff about visitor needs, operational constraints, and maintenance capabilities.

Key Insights:

  • 89% of staff have observed accessibility challenges among visitors
  • 73% feel inadequately trained to assist visually impaired visitors
  • 95% support implementation of enhanced accessibility features
  • 82% prefer low-maintenance, durable solutions
