Solution Justification

Evidence-Based Design Decisions

Braille Integration

Rationale: 10% of blind adults read braille, representing immediate independence for experienced users. Tactile information provides instant access without technology dependence.

Digital Accessibility

Rationale: 46.9% of severely visually impaired people use smartphones. QR codes provide scalable, updatable content delivery without infrastructure changes.

Universal Design Approach

Rationale: Solutions benefit all visitors, not just those with visual impairments. Enhanced signages improves both the clarity and completeness of information presented.

Analysis of Pre-Existing Solutions

Current Accessibility Landscape

Our research identified several existing approaches to botanical garden accessibility, each with specific limitations that our solution addresses:

Audio Tours (Traditional)

Limitations: Require equipment rental, limited content updates, not location-specific, expensive to maintain.

Our Improvement: QR code system uses visitors' own devices, provides unlimited content updates, and text to speech for the plants information.

Large Print Signage

Limitations: Only assists low vision users, doesn't address complete blindness, limited information density.

Our Improvement: Braille Guidebooks and Brailled Signs serves both low vision and blind users with comprehensive information access.

Guided Tours Only

Limitations: Scheduled dependency, limited availability, reduces visitor autonomy, not personalized.

Our Improvement: Braille Signages enables independent exploration at visitor's own pace and interests.

Project Context & Goals

The Challenge

Glasgow Botanic Gardens, while beautiful and educational, presented significant accessibility barriers for visually and auditory impaired visitors. Traditional signage and information systems were primarily visual, limiting the ability of these visitors to fully engage with the botanical collections and educational content.

Our Mission

To create inclusive, innovative solutions that enable visually and auditory impaired visitors to independently explore, learn about, and enjoy the botanical gardens through tactile, auditory, and digital accessibility enhancements.

Impact Goals

  • Improve accessibility for the visually and auditory impaired visitors.
  • Create replicable solutions for other botanical institutions
  • Enhance the overall visitor experience for all guests
  • Establish Glasgow Botanic Gardens as a model for inclusive design
